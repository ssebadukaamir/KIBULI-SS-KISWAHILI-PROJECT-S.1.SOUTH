Detailed Notes on Kiswahili Hesabu

1. Introduction to Kiswahili Numbers: Numbers (idadi) form the core of daily communication in Kiswahili, from telling time, dates, shopping, to social interaction. Learning numbers improves listening, speaking, reading, and writing skills according to the Kiswahili syllabus.

2. Basic Cardinal Numbers: 1 - moja | 2 - mbili | 3 - tatu | 4 - nne | 5 - tano | 6 - sita | 7 - saba | 8 - nane | 9 - tisa | 10 - kumi

3. Formation of Numbers Above Ten: Combine tens and units using "na" (and). Examples: 11 = kumi na moja, 15 = kumi na tano, 20 = ishirini, 21 = ishirini na moja, 30 = thelathini.

4. Larger Numbers: 100 = mia moja, 200 = mia mbili, 1000 = elfu moja. They combine, e.g., 1215 = elfu moja mia mbili na kumi na tano.

5. Using Numbers in Sentences: Numbers agree with noun classes (upatanishi). Example: Nina vitabu viwili. (I have two books.)

6. Days, Months, and Time: Days: Jumatatu, Jumanne... Months: Januari, Februari... Time telling uses numbers: saa moja (one o’clock).

7. Practical Applications: Counting objects, money, buying and selling, exchanging phone numbers, and dates.

Mnemonic Tip: “[Moja, Mbili, Tatu](pplx://action/translate) - count with me, so it’s easy!”

Why Learn Kiswahili Numbers? Mastery enhances communication skills essential for academics, commerce, and social interaction in East Africa.
